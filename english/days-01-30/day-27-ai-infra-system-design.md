## Day 27: Energy Efficiency and Thermal Management in AI Datacenters

### 1) Topic and Core Examination Areas
- **Topic**: Energy Efficiency and Thermal Management.
- **Core Examination Areas**: Power consumption of AI clusters, cooling technologies, and PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness).

### 2) Requirement Clarification and Metric Definitions
- **PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness)**: Ratio of total facility power to IT equipment power. Target < 1.3 for modern AI datacenters.
- **GPU Power Draw**: e.g., H100 SXM5 draws up to 700W per GPU.

### 3) Core Architecture/Technical Component Design
- **Cooling Systems**: Air cooling vs. Liquid cooling (direct-to-chip, immersion cooling) for high-density AI racks.
- **Power Distribution**: High-capacity PDUs (Power Distribution Units) and redundant power supplies.

### 4) Deep Dive into Key Technologies and Possible Solutions
- **Liquid Cooling**: More efficient than air cooling for high-density GPU racks, allowing higher TDP per rack and reducing PUE.
- **Dynamic Power Scaling**: Adjusting GPU power limits based on workload to save energy during low utilization.

### 5) Trade-off analysis
- **Cooling Cost vs. Density**: Liquid cooling has higher upfront infrastructure cost but enables higher GPU density and lower PUE, reducing long-term operational costs. Air cooling is cheaper to install but limits density and increases PUE.

### 6) How to determine the optimal solution
- For large-scale AI clusters (>1000 GPUs), liquid cooling (direct-to-chip or immersion) is increasingly necessary to manage thermal loads and achieve acceptable PUE. For smaller deployments, advanced air cooling may suffice.

### 7) Glossary: Full names and explanations of nouns and abbreviations
- **PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness)**: A metric measuring the efficiency of a datacenter, calculated as Total Facility Power / IT Equipment Power.
- **PDU (Power Distribution Unit)**: A device that distributes power to multiple equipment racks or servers.
- **TDP (Thermal Design Power)**: The maximum heat output generated by a component, used to size cooling systems.
